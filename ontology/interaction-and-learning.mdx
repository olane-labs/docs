### How Self-Improvement Works

The ontology doesn't just store knowledge — it learns from itself. This happens through a continuous feedback loop with several layers:

**1. Decision Traces — The Raw Material**

Every time the system acts — answering a question, scoring confidence, resolving an entity — the rationale and outcome are recorded as a **decision trace**. These traces form the observational foundation: a verifiable history of what the system did, why, and what happened next.

But traces alone are just a log. Self-improvement requires what happens *on top* of them.

**2. Pattern Recognition & Co-occurrence Analysis**

The system analyzes traces in aggregate to surface patterns invisible in any single decision. When entities repeatedly appear together, when certain behaviors co-occur across different contexts, or when confidence gaps cluster around specific domains — these signals emerge from cross-trace analysis, not from individual observations.

**3. Knowledge Artifacts — Distilled Insights**

Patterns that prove stable are packaged into **knowledge artifacts**: reusable insights that inform future decisions at a higher level of abstraction than raw traces. This is how the system moves from "I saw this happen" to "I know this is generally true."

**4. Gap Identification & Targeted Learning**

The system generates **targeted learning questions** — specific things that, when answered, will close gaps and raise confidence. This is proactive: the ontology identifies what it *doesn't* know and prioritizes closing those gaps.

**The Result**

Every ingestion deepens the graph. Every query reveals where gaps remain. Every decision trace feeds back into pattern recognition. The ontology gets smarter over time without manual curation.

Day 1 understanding of your codebase is fundamentally different from day 30 — and the improvement is automatic.
